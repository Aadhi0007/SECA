SECA vs LLaMA Architecture

Overview

Meta's LLaMA focuses on creating efficient, open-weights foundation models through extreme data scaling (trillions of tokens). SECA takes a completely different route: it focuses on finding the structural minimum required to solve specific cognitive pathways, eschewing foundation pre-training entirely.

Key Differences

Feature LLaMA Models SECA Engine
Knowledge Source Vast internet text (Pre-training) Evolutionary Search (Task-specific adaptation)
Openness Open-weights (Model fixed after training) Open-architecture (Self-modifying)
Hardware Req Multi-GPU setups for fine-tuning Can evolve on consumer hardware
Domain Focus General-purpose language Highly specific custom topologies
